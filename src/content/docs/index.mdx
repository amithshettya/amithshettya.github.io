I'm a Software Engineer who genuinely enjoys the puzzle of how data moves through a network. I recently wrapped up my Master's at Carnegie Mellon , where I spent most of my time obsessing over software development and the intricate architecture of distributed systems.

Right now, I'm at Cloudflare , building the kind of infrastructure that keeps the internet stable and secure. I spend most of my days in Go and Rust , solving the hidden scaling problems that happen behind the scenes of DDoS protection and WAN. For me, the best part is taking a messy, complex system and making it simple and efficient without anyone ever noticing the effort involved.

When I'm not diving into the Linux kernel or debugging distributed services, I'm usually deep in a different kind of logic. I've got a massive soft spot for good math and physics abstraction, and I also enjoy following geopolitics and economic events. If I'm not doing any of those, I'm likely overthinking my next move in chess—or, more realistically, just binge-watching anime.
